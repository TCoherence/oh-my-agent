# Finance Watchlist

## Default daily finance coverage

Every finance daily should explicitly look at:

1. China macro and policy
2. US macro and policy
3. US market volatility and risk appetite
4. China / Hong Kong market pulse
5. China property policy, housing finance, and credit-related signals
6. Tracked holdings over the last 7 days
7. Market / index-fund implications

## Source families

### US market volatility / risk appetite

Use a mix of:

- primary / official
  - Fed, Treasury, BLS, BEA, CBOE if relevant
- media / analysis
  - Reuters, Bloomberg, AP, FT, WSJ, CNBC when useful
- market data references
  - major index / ETF context pages when publicly readable

### China / Hong Kong market pulse

Use a mix of:

- primary / official
  - CSRC, PBOC, NBS, State Council, exchange notices when relevant
- media / analysis
  - Reuters, Bloomberg, Caixin, 财新, 证券时报, 上证报 when high-signal and cross-checkable

### China property policy / financing signals

Use a mix of:

- primary / official
  - PBOC, NBS, Ministry of Housing and Urban-Rural Development, Ministry of Finance, State Council, local-government housing policy notices
- media / analysis
  - Reuters, Bloomberg, Caixin and other high-signal housing-policy reporting

## Finance vs politics boundary

Use this split consistently:

- `finance`
  - housing policy as a market / credit / financing / demand signal
  - how policy changes affect spreads, sentiment, liquidity, financing conditions, developers, and household demand
- `politics`
  - legislative or administrative framing, political intent, security framing, top-level geopolitical implications

If the same policy appears in both domains:

- finance should not repeat politics prose
- finance should translate the policy into market impact and financing implications
